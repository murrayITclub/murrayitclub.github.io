You've been working hard on your website. Now it's time for other people to be able to see it from any computer! Here's how to publish:

Before you start, you'll need to be totally sure you know your @stpaul.k12.mn.us email address. If you aren't 100% sure, ask first!

1. We'll use a tool called Github for hosting our websites. Click here to go to Github.

2. You'll need to start by creating an account. Your username will become part of your website's address, and your email needs to be typed perfectly. Fill out the form, then click Sign up for GitHub

3. Next, you'll see a few questions. You can click whichever options you want; here are my suggestions. Make some choices, then click Complete setup

4. A few more quick setup steps: rotate a picture to prove you're a human. Use the arrows until it's the right way, then click Done. You'll also want to uncheck the box, then click Next: Select a plan.

5. Click Choose Free.

6. Next, you'll probably see something like this.

You'll need to check your email - click here to sign in on the computer (don't use your iPad or phone). Type your s-number and AZZ password.

7. Look for an email like this:

If you didn't get the email, first check your Spam folder. If it's not there, ask for help!

Click the email to open it, then click Verify email address. After you click the button, you'll probably need to Sign in.

8. Now you're finally ready to publish your website! Just a few more steps. First, click +, then New repository:

9. Under Repository name, carefully type your username, then .github.io (all as one word - no spaces). Then click Create repository.

10. You should see something like this. Click uploading an existing file.

Then click choose your files.

When this box appears, hold the control button on your keyboard, then click all of the files in your Everything IT folder. Then click Open

Wait until all the files finish loading, then click Commit changes

11. You'll see a loading screen like this.

Once the loading screen disappears, you can go to [your username].github.io to see your website! Try it, or even better - have a friend take a look.

Congratulations - you've built and published your first website! Check off step 10, and be SO proud of yourself.
