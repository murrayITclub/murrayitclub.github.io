Photo Editing

Project #3: Pixlr Final Project

Goals: be able to...
  • Choose and independently follow a tutorial
  • Learn more about working with layers
  • Explore other tools as you need them

There are TONS of things it's possible to do with Pixlr. Now that you've learned some of the basics, you have the skills to choose a tutorial and follow it to learn whatever you are most interested in!

Options:

Green Screening:
adding a fun background to an image

Photo Letters:
making text look like it's cut out of an image

Partial Filter:
Make an image black and white with patches of color

If there's something else you REALLY want to learn, talk to Eileen about finding another tutorial!



Project #2: Use Pixlr to "carve" a pumpkin (or create a custom emoji)

Goals: be able to...
  • Use all three selection tools
  • Copy and paste from one image to another
  • Use free transform to adjust parts
  • Work with layers

Start by saving one of these two base images plus other pictures you want to take parts of.
Then use Pixlr to bring them together!




How do I do it?

Part 1:
Saving images in your club folder.

Part 2:
Putting the pieces together in Pixlr

When you're done...

  1. Make sure your creation is saved...
    • as a .PNG
    • with a useful name
    • in your folder for this club
  2. Keep expanding your Pixlr knowledge by...
    • Use selection tools to put yourself into a painting or famous photo
    • Choosing a photo and exploring what other Pixlr tools can do
    • Try making a Keep Calm poster
      (click File->New, then set width to 800 and height to 1200)
    • Try a tutorial: creating a hybrid animal or putting a picture in words










Project #1: learning to use Pixlr to create memes about class expectations.

Goals: be able to...
  • Find and save an image from the Internet
  • Open an image in Pixlr
  • Add text to an image
  • Save the image from Pixlr to a computer

You can find your own image, or use one of these:

Meme images

To save a picture, right-click (control-click or two-finger-tap), then choose Save Image As.

When you're done...

  1. Post your meme on our class board.
  2. Keep expanding your Pixlr knowledge by...
    • Making more memes for other classes or just for fun
    • Try making a Keep Calm poster
      (click File→New, then set width to 800 and height to 1200)
    • Try a tutorial: creating a hybrid animal or putting a picture in words
